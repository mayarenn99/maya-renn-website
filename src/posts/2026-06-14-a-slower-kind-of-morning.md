title: A slower kind of morning
description: The five-minute ritual that changed how my whole day feels — no products, no plan, just light.
date: 2026-06-14
---

For a long time I thought a good morning routine needed *equipment*. A journal with prompts. A specific tea. An app that hummed at me until I meditated.

Then one grey Tuesday I did none of it. I opened the window, stood there while the kettle worked, and watched the street wake up. Five minutes, maybe less. It was the best morning I'd had in months.

## The ritual, such as it is

- Open a window before you open a screen.
- Let the kettle be the timer. While it heats, do nothing on purpose.
- Drink the first cup somewhere with light, even if the light is grey.

That's the whole thing. It isn't impressive, and that's the point. Routines fail when they ask you to become someone else before 7 a.m. This one only asks you to be where you already are.

> Slowness isn't doing things at a lower speed. It's doing fewer things at their actual speed.
