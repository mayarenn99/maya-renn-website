The loveliest rooms I know all have one thing in common, and it isn't a colour or a sofa. It's *air*. Space between objects. A shelf that isn't full. A wall that's allowed to be quiet.

## The one-question edit

Walk through a room and ask each thing a single question: **would I choose you again today?**

Not "might you be useful someday" — that question hoards. Not "did you cost money" — that question guilts. Just: chosen again, today, yes or no. The nos go in a box. If you don't miss the box in a month, you never open it.

## What stays gets seen

Here's the quiet magic: when a shelf holds three objects instead of eleven, you actually *see* all three. The clay vase becomes a small daily pleasure instead of visual noise. Editing isn't about owning less; it's about noticing more.

> A room breathes when everything in it could be pointed to with a small story.

## Start with one surface

Not the whole house. One shelf, one sill, one table. Let it stay clear for a week and watch how often your eyes go there to rest.

---

My cleared surface this week: the windowsill, down to a jug of sweet peas and nothing else. The nothing else is doing most of the work.
