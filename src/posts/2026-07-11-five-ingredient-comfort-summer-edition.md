I promised the tomato version when summer committed, and this week it did — all at once, the way summer does. So: same pot, same lazy arithmetic, five ingredients that don't ask much of a warm evening.

## Tomato & white bean, barely cooked

1. Warm olive oil with two cloves of garlic, sliced thin. Stop before brown — you know this part.
2. Add a punnet of cherry tomatoes, halved, and a pinch of salt. Let them slump for five minutes until they're more sauce than salad.
3. In go the white beans, liquid and all. Simmer five more.
4. Off the heat: torn basil and your best olive oil, the one you hide from guests.

Eat it lukewarm on purpose. In July, soup that doesn't insist on being hot is a kindness.

## The real recipe

The five ingredients are the excuse. The actual recipe is this: cook something short enough that you're still willing to set the table, light the stub of a candle, and sit down like it's an occasion. It counts even when it's Tuesday. Especially then.
