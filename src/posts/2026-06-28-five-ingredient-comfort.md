Some evenings deserve a recipe that reads like a short poem. This is my most-repeated one: white beans, good olive oil, garlic, rosemary, lemon. Twenty minutes, one pot, no decisions.

## White bean & rosemary soup

1. Warm a generous pour of olive oil. Add three cloves of garlic, sliced thin, and a sprig of rosemary. Let them get friendly, not brown.
2. Add two jars (or tins) of white beans, liquid and all, plus a splash of water.
3. Simmer ten minutes. Crush some beans against the pot until it's as creamy as you like.
4. Off the heat: lemon juice, salt, more oil on top.

Serve with bread you tore instead of sliced. Eat it somewhere soft.

## Why five ingredients

It isn't minimalism for its own sake. It's that a short recipe leaves room in the evening for the parts that aren't cooking — the table setting, the candle you keep meaning to burn, the conversation that only happens when nobody's rushing back to the stove.
